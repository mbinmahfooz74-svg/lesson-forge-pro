Workspace Administrator's Manual

Your company has its own Lesson Forge workspace: a private AI engine that watches your field, builds your curricula, and produces training material carrying your company's name and colors — in English and Arabic. This manual covers everything a workspace administrator does.

1 · What your workspace is

  • A private space: your fields, sources, courses, and files are visible only to your team (and the platform operator, for support). Never to other companies.
  • A self-building engine: describe a training field in one sentence and it constructs the topic map, glossary (English/Arabic), and a proposed course catalog.
  • A materials factory: every session becomes a complete pack — slides, assessment with rubric, learner handouts — branded for your company.
  • A living library: the engine scans your field weekly and proposes updates, so material never goes stale.

2 · Setting up (first 30 minutes)

  1. Create the workspace at /en/workspace/new — company name, your name, email, password. You become the first admin.
  2. Sign in at /en/login. Use /ar/… anywhere for the Arabic interface.
  3. Create your first field (§4) and add 3–5 good sources (§5) — the engine's quality tracks the quality of what you feed it.
  4. Plan your first course (§6) and generate a pack — you'll have branded slides within the hour.

3 · Your team & seats

Open Workspace in the sidebar:

  • Invite a member: enter their email, pick Admin or Member, send. They receive an email with an accept link (valid 7 days), set their password, and land in your workspace.
  • Seats: the header shows usage (e.g. 3 / 5). Members + pending invitations count against the limit; expired invitations free their seat automatically. Need more seats? Contact the platform operator.
  • Invite at least one second Admin so the workspace is never locked to a single person.

4 · Training fields (verticals)

A field is a subject area the engine owns end-to-end — e.g. Workplace safety & compliance, Retail customer excellence, Cybersecurity awareness.

  1. Go to Verticals → type the field name and one sentence describing scope and audience → Create & self-build.
  2. Within minutes the field page shows: a taxonomy (topic areas), a proposed course catalog (in your Review queue), and an English/Arabic glossary of the field's terms.
  3. The weekly scan starts automatically: every week the engine checks what changed in this field and files proposals for your review.
Scope well"Fire safety for site supervisors in industrial facilities" builds a far better field than "Safety". Name the audience in the description.

5 · Feeding it sources

Source pipeline → paste any URL, pick the field, Ingest. Articles, YouTube videos (with captions), PDF reports, regulations — all work. Each capture gets a quality score; weak captures are flagged so you know what the engine is standing on. Failures show exactly why and offer a Retry.

What to feed itYour internal policy documents (published at a URL), industry regulations, the best explainer articles you know, conference talks. 5–10 strong sources per field is a solid base; add more anytime.

6 · Courses & materials

  1. Courses → type a topic, pick the field, Plan course. The engine designs a multi-session arc: objectives per session (mapped to Bloom's learning levels), durations, prerequisites.
  2. Open the course. Per session, click Draft — you get a minute-by-minute educator guide: timed agenda, talking points, analogies, anticipated learner questions.
  3. Click Generate materials — four files appear: slides (PPTX), assessment + marking rubric (DOCX), learner handout (DOCX and PDF). All carry your company name and brand color.
  4. Not happy with a draft? Redraft regenerates it — and gets better as your feedback accumulates (§8).

7 · The review queue

The engine never publishes on its own — everything it proposes waits in Review queue, sorted by significance:

  • New topic proposals (from field-building and the weekly scan): approving one automatically plans its course.
  • Weekly briefings: approve to accept the field update.
  • Reject anything that's noise — rejections sharpen future proposals.

Make the queue a 10-minute weekly habit — Monday morning works well; the weekly scan runs before dawn on Mondays.

8 · Making it learn your voice

After a trainer delivers a session, open Feedback, choose the field, and paste any of: the session transcript, quick ratings, or debrief notes (what worked / what flopped / what learners asked). The engine distills this into its skill memory — visible, versioned, on the same page — and every future draft in your workspace inherits it. Two or three sessions of feedback noticeably shift the material toward your trainers' style.

9 · Privacy & branding

  • Isolation: your data is scoped to your workspace on every page, action, and file download. Other companies cannot see it, ever.
  • Branding: your company name and accent color appear on every slide deck, assessment, and handout automatically — set at signup; ask the platform operator to change them.
  • Arabic: the full interface works in Arabic (/ar), glossaries are bilingual by default, and Arabic handout editions render right-to-left correctly.
